About Massage

Relax, forget your worries and get all the stress and knots taken away from the body. Whether it be the comfort of your own home or a spa, a massage can be one of the most pampering experience.

For most of us a regular massage is no longer a luxury but a necessity. Hectic lifestyle, sitting on a chair in office all day (often with a bad posture) or those frequent travels - all take their toll on our body. Regular massage treatments prevent the formation of knots and help repair tired muscles - which can go a long way in avoiding chronic back problems.

When should one get a massage - simple answer is there is always time for a little treat. But if you ever feel that your back or legs are tired, or you can hear 'creaks' while moving or have that nagging muscle pain, see a qualified massage therapist as soon as possible.

And finally an interesting finding for the ladies - regular massage sessions can also help to delay and reduce the onset of cellulite. The therapeutic oils used in the massage treatment hydrates your skin providing it with the essential nutrients.

Massage Treatments offered by BeautyHome
  • Aromatherapy Massage:
    A de-stressing massage with the use of essential oils. The massage therapist can select oils that are relaxing, energizing, stress-reducing or balancing.
  • Back and Shoulders Massage:
    This shorter massage is a good solution if you haven’t got enough time for a full body massage. Most of us experience soreness and pain in the upper body from time to time, hence the Back and Shoulders Massage will ease concentrated aches and pains, discomfort or tension in this area of the body.
  • Deep Tissue Massage:
    Deep tissue massage is used for chronically tight or painful muscles, repetitive strain, postural problems, or recovery from injury. The massage therapist uses slower strokes or friction techniques across the grain of the muscle.
  • Indian Head Massage:
    The focus is on the head and neck. The therapist will use a combination of hard and gentle strokes to give you one of the most relaxing experience you'll ever have. It improves blood circulation and reduces stress.
  • Pregnancy Massage:
    This massage is increasingly popular with expectant mothers. In addition to relieving back pain, pregnancy massage is used to reduce stress, decrease swelling and reduce anxiety and depression.
  • Swedish Massage:
    In a Swedish massage the massage therapist performs various massage strokes. These movements warm up the muscle tissue, releasing tension and gradually breaking up muscle ‘knots’ or adhered tissues. Swedish massage reduces muscle pain and promotes relaxation.

Massage - Beauty Tips

  • Always choose your therapist carefully - having a qualified and knowledgeable therapist can make a big difference to your treatment experience
  • Do not have a heavy meal before having a massage
  • Drink plenty of water after the massage: the process of removing 'knots' removes harmful toxins from the body. Drink plenty of water to flush them out
  • If you have any injury or regular pains, inform the therapist prior to the massage. A qualified therapist would either know the right treatment or refer you to a specialist
