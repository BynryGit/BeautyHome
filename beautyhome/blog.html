Why does interval training work?


We are all probably aware of the benefits of interval training - its considered superior to constant intensity workouts for building stamina, improving fitness and fat loss. In fact research has shown that Tabata - which is a form of intense interval training - can burn as much calories via a four minute routine as an hour of cycling. All sounds very good and numerous studies have confirmed that interval training does work. But what's the science behind interval training workouts.

Let's start with the basics - how does our body burn fat. Our body stores energy in the form of fat as a safety mechanism against adverse periods where the body may not get sufficient fuel. The survival instincts of the body mean that we are predisposed to store fat. That's why my basic theory that if you want to burn fat then your ‘calories out’ has to be greater than ‘calories in’.

But we should not forget that our body is the most perfect machine ever created. If you reduce calories in - it adapts and lowers the metabolic rate. Even if you do the same exercise everyday the body adapts and the benefits of exercise start plateauing out.

Fat is stored as triglycerides in the fat cells - which when in need gets converted to fatty acids which can be oxidised by our body. Given the fact that the body is evolved to preserve energy it uses two mechanisms to slow down fat burn - adapting to a new exercise or diet regime and second producing lactic acid while exercising which naturally slows down exercising capacity of the muscles.

Interval training addresses both the mechanisms. It combines short bursts of high intensity training along with periods of rest. Since the body is alternating between high and low intensity workouts it 'confuses' the muscles - preventing the body from adapting to the workout.

HIIT combines anaerobic and aerobic periods of exercise. In the anaerobic period the body is starved of oxygen and hence it burns the fat in the body. While in the aerobic period the body refuels with oxygen. Normally, the body fights long periods of anaerobic activity by forming lactic acid in the muscles which causes them to become sore and slow down.

However, when we are doing interval training, the high intensity part helps to breakdown triglycerides and burn fat, while the low intensity part prevents the formation of lactic acid. Thus the body can burn fat for longer periods of time. And since the muscles are not able to adapt to the change in intensity, the body does not fight the burning of fat.

The other effect is the after-burn. Put simply it means that the body can continue burning calories even after the exercise. Studies have shown that both interval training and resistance training are far superior to plain running or cycling in burning more calories after the exercise.

So next time you plan a gym session, go for circuit training or HIIT and melt away those fat cells !!

Should woman do weight training?


The question faces most of the regular women gym goers - with lots of reasons being cited as to why they should not. You don’t want to bulk, it might destroy your figure, it can cause back pain and other injuries, its bad for the joints and so on. Thus a large portion of women stick to pilates, yoga and running. The benefits of weight training, particularly as women age, far outweigh cardio training. Here are some reasons why weight training is good for women

  • You will not bulk up: Despite the misconceptions, the hormonal differences between men and women mean that resistance training does not imply then women will bulk up. Yes you will gain lean muscle and improve muscle tone, but you’ll need to take supplements or spend hours in the gym to really bulk up
  • Weight training will add to your curves: With weight training you improve muscle tone and lose fat - perfect for your figure. With cardio or diet regime you would loose muscle as well as fat - not so good for the curves
  • Resistance training is better for loosing weight: Numerous studies have shown that resistance training is superior to cardio for after-burn - implying that your body can burn calories for longer after you finish exercising. And more muscle mass means that the body’s metabolic rate increases - hence more fat burn
  • It improves bone density: Regular weight training strengthens the bones and improves bond density - particularly important for women as they age. It reduces the risk of osteoporosis.
  • It keeps the heart healthy: Studies have reported that regular weight training reduces risks of heart disease. It has been shown that the blood pressure levels are lower among weight training subjects the following day compared to pure cardio training
  • Reduces stress level: While any form of exercise has been associated with reduced stress levels, it has been shown that people who weight train regularly can manage stressful situations better than those who don’t
  • It lowers the risk of injury: Stronger muscles and give more support to your joints reducing the risk of any injury. Make sure you do the exercises properly. Check the exercise videos at trainmefit.co.uk for exercise videos to do weight training with the correct form.

Choosing the right facial


Going to a spa is pure bliss - time to relax and unwind. Last weekend, while visiting one of the spas just outside London, I decided to opt for a facial treatment. The jargon of the treatments on offer was mind boggling - lots of ‘re’ in the descriptions: relaxing, rejuvenating, refreshing, rehydrating, revitalising, rebalancing, renewing, relieving, regenerating, restoring just to name a few. This got me brooding on what is the right facial for me? First the basics - the objective of a facial treatment is to improve skin tone and make you feel pampered and relaxed. It removes the dead cells from the skin, nourishes the face and leaves your skin glowing and healthy.

For many people, including me, the objective of a facial is to be pampered and have a healthier skin. However for some the objectives may differ based on You want a deep cleanse of the skin Your skin is blotchy and tired and you want an even skin tone You want to rehydrate the skin You want to fight those signs of ageing and want to firm and lift the skin

So what facial should you choose? Almost all facials will involve the basis steps - cleansing, exfoliating, a mask, a moisturizer and massage of the face and neck. Luxury facials would typically be for longer and involve multiple masks and more pampering massage of the face, neck and shoulders. The key to choose the right facial is a) your current skin condition b) the kind of mask you want and c) whether to go for steaming and extraction. If your aim is a deep cleanse, go for facials that include words like detox, refresh, revitalise or maybe some form of oxygen treatment. Choose a mask that is deep cleansing and you may opt for blackhead removal exfoliation. Some spas offer oxygen jets which would help to cleanse your skin. If you are prone to acne, be careful about using the classic lotions as these can cause a breakout. Instead go for a cleanser with salicylic acid, a clay mask, and a lactic acid peel. Make sure the therapist avoids scrubs; applying these can irritate the skin To improve skin tone and rehydrate the skin opt for moisturising masks. Longer exfoliation would be good for promoting an even skin tone. To fight wrinkles, pick a facial with a little extra exfoliation. Glycolic, lactic, and enzyme peels are all great ways to get rid of that layer of dead skin that makes pores look larger and wrinkles look deeper. For dull skin, look for a facial that combines a mild peel and brightening ingredients such as vitamin C or kojic acid. Lastly, extraction and steaming has become very popular to remove blotches and pimply spots on the skin. Steaming softens the pores, allowing extraction to work. My advice, do not use an extractor machine as these can cause discolorations. Instead ask the therapist to use a gauze - but avoid too much pressure as it can break the capillaries. Finally if you have very dry skin or eczema skip the steaming as it can exacerbate the condition and use a warm cloth instead Whatever your reason for going for a facial, sit back relax and feel thoroughly pampered !!

The Myth of ‘fat free’, ‘sugar free’ and ‘gluten free’ diets


In today’s busy lifestyle, we do our best to remain fit and healthy. A number of ‘free’ diets have sprung up - gluten free, sugar free, fat free, lactose free. While some of these are beneficial for people with intolerance, a number of them are more marketing strategies - its not entirely obvious that they are good.

Just because a food is labelled fat free - its may not superior to a more ‘normal’ product that has fat in it. A number of studies have shown that moderate amount of fat is good for you. The first question is what kid of fat are you eating - monounsaturated fats are actually beneficial for the body - fish oils, fats from nuts and fruits like avocado contain essential oils and nutrients that keep your body healthy. The second question is what has been substituted to make the product fat free. Often the carbohydrate content has been increased to substitute for fat - which may do more harm than good. Adding artificial sweeteners again - does more harm than good. Unless you have a medical condition - don’t go for sweeteners. Or the amount of processed food may be higher in ‘fat free’ products - again it may be doing more harm than good. A similar case can be made for sugar free or gluten free products. Lets compare some of the common misconceptions of ‘free’ products that we often consume

Sugar free 'diet' drinks It’s been well documented that fizzy drinks are not good for your health. However, if one wants to have a coke - is it better to have a ‘diet’ one or as one of friends call a ‘full fat one’. Its not obvious which is a better choice. While having a ‘diet’ cola does avoid refined sugar intake, some studies have shows that having sweeteners may increase your sugar cravings. So overall your daily consumption of sugar may actually go up. Other studies have shown that some sweeteners may be carcinogenic though the research appears inconclusive. Diet soda contains the same amount of acid as a regular one - which harms your teeth and your body ph balance. In a study involving 2465 subjects over 9 years, scientists found that regular consumption of diet soda increased risk of heart stroke by 48%. Conclusion: Moderation is the key. Don’t fool yourself that having a ‘diet’ soda means that you’re going for the healthier option

Gluten free diets While gluten free is becoming the new fad diet - we must understand what exactly gluten free means. Gluten is a form of protein found in wheat, rye and barley. So going gluten free is giving up the consumption of predominantly wheat and wheat based products. The good thing - most processed foods are based on wheat and sugar - cakes, pastries, white bread - all of which contribute to weight gain. So if you’re on a gluten free diet you give up all these. However, unless you are gluten intolerant or have been advised by your doctor to avoid gluten - all the gluten free diet is providing a framework to avoid processed food. Something that can be achieved by a healthy diet and less complications. Beware of substitutes that go for a gluten free diet. Sometimes it could be processed corn - again not obvious thats good for you Conclusion: Unless the doctor advises, gluten free diet is just a framework for avoiding processed food. Something that can be done with much more easily with a balanced diet

Fat free foods Walk along a super store and food with labels of ‘fat free’, ‘low fat’, ‘reduced fat’ pop up. Some of it is good - skinny latte retains most of the goodness of milk, while cutting down on fat. But in some cases to make products low fat - substitutes include sugar, thickeners and strange it may sound but salt - all of which can do more harm than good. Our body needs fat - a healthy diet should have 30% of our body energy via fat. The key is to differentiate between good and bad fat. Good fats include both monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats.Monounsaturated fats (like olive oils) lower the LDL "bad cholesterol" in the bloodstream and raise the amount of HDL "good cholesterol." Polyunsaturated fats found in fatty fish such as tuna and salmon also help lower LDL cholesterol. Bad fats include saturated fat (found in animal products like beef, pork) artificial trans fats, found in partially hydrogenated oils. Key to healthy diet is to encourage healthy fats in the diet - nuts, fish, olive oil are all good for you. Conclusion: Don't go for fat free food products, choose good fat products. And if you feel like a treat sometimes, don't feel guilty - just keep it in moderation.

    The many benefits of fasting


    Over recent years, the health community has taken to the idea of fasting. Diets like the 5:2 diets have gained in popularity which advocate fasting (read as less than 25% of your normal calorie intake) for 2 days and eating a normal diet for the remaining 5 days. Health spas which encourage people to fast and enjoy a healthy week of yoga and detox are fast gaining popularity.

    The concept of fasting is definitely not new - infact it is something that almost all religions advocate - Christianity advocates the concept of lent which typically involves giving up something on the food side (more likely than not to be alcohol or chocolates or crisps - and hence helps to detox the body). Islam advocates fasting for the month of Ramadan. Hinduism has various references of fasting and ancient yogic scriptures have advocated one day of only fruits and vegetables diet. Probably the religious doctrines knew more about the human body than we do - probably an effect of the technology era we live in which effectively distances our thinking process from what our body is telling us. A number of researches have verified the positive aspects of fasting - from the most intuitive ones of aiding in weight loss, but more importantly to help is various diseases including arthritis, blood pressure and even making your skin gorgeous and beautiful.

    So how does fasting help

    • The simplest benefit of fasting in reducing the calorie intake. To me the essence of various diets is calories in - calories out. If you want to loose weight, either reduce the calories in or increase calories out. Simple things in life are good
    • Fasting is the best way to detox the body. Lets face it - despite our best efforts, we can’t avoid processed food - something that our body is not really used to. So a day (or two) of fasting gives our digestive system the much needed rest and helps to eliminate the toxins from the body
    • Fasting promotes better eating. When you switch over a day of dietary requirements to fruits, vegetables and other natural stuff - you’re taking in a lot of nutrients - something that you may have generally avoided. One of my colleagues skilfully avoids any leaves that come along with his main course.
    • It helps your skin as well. Lower levels of free radicals and toxins in the body implies better skin tone. Not only that, with the digestive system given a bit of rest, the body can focus energy in regenerating other parts of the body - including not just the internal organs but also the dead skin cells.
    • Recent researches have shown the benefits of fasting beyond the above obvious points. It has been shown to slow the onset of Alzheimers, cancer and heart diseases.
    • During fasting, the brain releases protein called BDNF which activates production of new neurons. This protein also protects your brain cells from changes associated with Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s disease
    • Regular fasting removes free radicals from the body which have been associated with increased risks of cancer. It also improves the immune system. Remember that in most of the animal kingdom - when they are sick, the natural tendency is to stop eating and letting the body repair itself
    • Strange it may sound but fasting can lead to increased energy levels. Once the body overcomes the initial stages of change in eating pattern, the energy used up in the digestive system is diverted to rest of the body making the mind clearer and feeling more energetic

    So go ahead and give your body the detox it deserves. I am in no way advocating a too low eating habit which can be characterised as close to being anorexic. I am a natural foodie and love eating. But once in a while, its good to give the body a break from the daily routine of eating.

    I am at a loss of words to describe the city - its simply amazing !! A city buzzing with life and a city of contrasts. Its steeped in culture and the people are extremely polite and welcoming. Yet its as modern as any European city. Technology wise probably more advanced - almost everywhere there is a wifi connection and one can see people with the latest gadgets in their hands. I’ve been told that the per person consumption of alcohol is one of the highest in the world and if you go to a club, be prepared to last till at least the early hours of the morning.

    But what struck me about the Koreans is that they all seem so fit and skinny. It was almost impossible to find anyone who one could categorise as fat - forget the obese category. Yet at every two minutes one would find a coffee shop selling mouth watering desserts - and one could see Koreans not being shy about eating.

    So what's the secret about Koreans being so fit and skinny (a disclaimer - my observations purely stem from seeing people in Seoul). Got chatting with some of the guests at the hotel and a bit of web searching later, some interesting observations

    • People are extremely conscious about with their looks and figure. No wonder their figures look toned and skin super healthy
    • Diet is dominated by sea food and very little fried stuff forms part of the meal.
    • Despite an abundance of shops catering for desserts, Korean diet has very little processed food. The concept of Organic is gaining more popularity in the west - but we forget that for most of Asia, the main diet has always been very close to natural organic stuff.
    • Korean diet consists of lots of vegetables. Almost every meal has an abundance of vegetables
    • Koreans are extremely emotional people with very strong family ties. A number of researches have shown that surrounding yourself with people makes one healthier and happier in life.
    • Lots of friends and family around makes you a social eater - where you tend to watch what you eat and in general eat less. One thing I noticed that most of the people eating in the restaurants were in groups rather than in twos.
    • The serving portions are smaller - even smaller than London and way smaller than the US. I ordered a steak with mashed potatoes and felt was a bit hungry even after my meal :)

    While I am not an advocate of being obsessed with looks or figure, there a number is things one can learn from the east - more vegetables, less processed food and a more social lifestyle can perhaps make us healthier and happier !

    Face cleanser


    Washing one’s face is a daily routine; it is pretty simple which should be done twice during the day in the morning and at night. A morning cleansing is beneficial; as it removes any toxins the skin eliminates during the night and refreshes the skin, preparing it for make-up application. It is

    important to cleanse your skin every night to remove make-up, impurities, dead skin cells, and excess oil.

    For the removal of eye make-up, especially waterproof mascara, there are particular products specifically formulated for the eye area. However, some gentle facial cleansers can effectively remove all make-up, eye, and facial, as well as cleanse the skin. Use an oil free cleanser for oily skin, and a milky or creamy cleanser for drier skin types.

    The original use of a toner is to return the skin's pH back to normal. It is also useful to complete the cleansing process, as it will remove any last traces of make-up and cleanser residue. Some toners have very beneficial ingredients that help certain skin conditions. As an example, ethanol will help with excess oil, whereas lactic acid or other alpha-hydroxy acid (AHA) will help exfoliate and smooth the skin's texture.

    This is one of the most important parts of your daily skin care routine, because toner lowers the skins pH level, calms the skin and helps prepare it for any of the night creams or serums that will follow. It also helps to balance the skin after cleansing and before applying a moisturising cream.

    Don’t skip the moisturizer as your skin needs to be replenished each night. Don’t forget to moisturise your lips as the skin over the lips is thinner than anywhere else on the face. Use a strong emollient-based product each night to keep your lips plump and moist. Along with the lips, don’t forget to hydrate the eye zone, with a very light product which is designed for this.

    A word of advice! It is important to choose moisturisers that include natural ingredients such as shea butter, cocoa butter, aloe vera, jojoba, rosehip, ginseng, seaweed and pure essential oils. These botanical extracts and oils helps to improve skin elasticity, texture, clarity, fine lines and wrinkles.

    Facial Scrub


    A good facial scrub helps to maintain the glow on your complexion and to avoid your blackheads growing.

    A face scrub is an exfoliation product which has been designed specifically for use on the face. The face sees a lot of wear and tear; it requires special attention when it comes to skincare. For this reason, it is important to use scrubs marked for use on the face in facial care, rather than generic exfoliating scrubs.

    Facial scrubs make way for new skin beneath every day. It helps to unclog pores from dirt, blackheads, dead skin and other surface impurities so that new, healthy skin may flourish. Exfoliation is considered one of the most important techniques that you can perform on your skin to resolve certain skin problems as well as to achieve healthy and glowing skin.

    There are two types of exfoliant "chemical" or "acid" and “physical.” In the chemical or acid exfoliant, you apply it, leave it on the skin, and it works to dissolve the dry skin cells. These exfoliant include ingredients such as Glycolic Acid, AHAs and BHA, and enzymes such as Pumpkin, Papaya, and Pineapple.  In a “physical" exfoliant you have to do the work. These exfoliant include facial brushes, sponges, and facial scrubs.

    The benefits of exfoliation

    Exfoliation helps to destroy acne-causing bacteria as it tends to dry out the skin and prevents future breakouts. It clears the red, dark marks that remain on the skin long after the breakout has healed. The key to fading those post-breakout marks is to increase your exfoliation. The result is more even-toned skin with less scarring.

    It clears clogged pores. Clogged pores are basically blackheads, whiteheads, and little clogged bumps on the skin, often on the forehead. The more you exfoliate; it will remove surface dry skin cells. As a result, less oil will stay trapped and congested in the pores.

    These are brown spots that come from age, pregnancy, hormonal changes, and genetics. Exfoliation is beneficial for breaking up the pigmented cells to allow them to fade.

    Exfoliation is very important during the winter months. Exfoliate to remove the dry skin cells, and then moisturize the new skin cells, resulting in a moister skin.
